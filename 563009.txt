As described by television historian Jake Austen , " Allen thought Presley was talentless and absurd ... [ he ] set things up so that Presley would show his contrition " . Allen , for his part , later wrote that he found Presley 's " strange , gangly , country - boy charisma , his hard - to - define cuteness , and his charming eccentricity intriguing " and simply worked the singer into the customary " comedy fabric " of his program .
